Introduction

Goals
Regarding Trustworthy LLMs, our goals include: (1) How to define Trustworthy LLMs, specifically, how to delineate the scope and boundaries for relevant work. It is also important to consider how to involve relevant datasets, tasks, and metrics to measure the trustworthiness of LLMs. (2) What is the performance of existing mainstream large language models in different trustworthy-related aspects? (3) From these evaluation results, can we identify any new insights to promote progress in future research?

Contribution
We have conducted a thorough and comprehensive exploration of the trustworthiness of large language models. Specifically, we introduce TrustLLM, which is divided into two parts: (1) Firstly, it includes guidelines for assessing the trustworthiness of LLMs, and based on these guidelines, we propose a classification system for LLM trustworthiness, encompassing eight aspects, including truthfulness, safety, fairness, robustness, privacy, machine ethics, transparency, and accountability. (2) We have established benchmarks for six of these aspects. This is a comprehensive and integrated benchmark, comprising over 15 subcategories, covering more than 25 datasets and 14 mainstream large language models.

Six Core aspects

Truthfulness

The accurate representation of information, facts, and results by an AI system.

Safety

The outputs from LLMs should only engage users in a safe and healthy conversation.

Fairness

The quality or state of being fair, especially fair or impartial treatment.

Robustness

The ability of a system to maintain its level of performance under a variety of circumstances.

Privacy

The norms and practices that help to safeguard human autonomy, identity, and dignity.

Machine Ethics

Ensuring moral behaviors of man-made machines that use artificial intelligence, otherwise known as artificial intelligent agents.
